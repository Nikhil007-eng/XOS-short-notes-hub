Lecture 5-6

Elimination Reactions

E1 Elimination

  • Carbocation intermediate
  • Zaitsev product major (more substituted)
  • Favored by: 3¬∞ RX, heat, polar protic solvents

E2 Elimination

  • Concerted one-step mechanism
  • Anti-periplanar geometry required
  • Zaitsev (small base) vs Hoffmann (bulky base)

Neighboring Group Participation (NGP)

  • Internal nucleophile assists
  • Retention via double inversion
  • Rate dramatically increases

Ethers & Haloarenes

Williamson Ether Synthesis

  • R-O‚Åª + R'-X ‚Üí R-O-R'
  • SN2 mechanism
  • Works best with 1¬∞ unbranched halides

Cleavage of Ethers

  • R-O-R' + HX ‚Üí R-OH + R'-X
  • SN2 (for 1¬∞ alkyl)
  • SN1 (for 3¬∞ or resonance-stabilized)

Haloarene Reactivity

  • Less reactive than haloalkanes due to:
  • 1. Resonance stabilization
  • 2. sp¬≤ C-X bond strength
  • 3. Phenyl cation instability

Nucleophilic Aromatic Substitution

  • Requires strong EWG at ortho/para
  • Meisenheimer complex intermediate
  • Rate: Ar-F > Ar-Cl > Ar-Br > Ar-I
