Key Reducing Agents

Reagent Nature & Selectivity Primary Use Solvent
LiAlH4 (LAH) Nucleophilic, Non-selective, Highly Reactive Reduces almost all carbonyls (C=O, N groups) to alcohols/amines. Polar Aprotic
NaBH4 (SBH) Nucleophilic, Mild & Selective Reduces only aldehydes & ketones. Polar Protic
DIBAL-H Electrophilic, Very Selective Reduces esters to aldehydes at -78°C. Non-polar
B2H6 (BH3.THF) Electrophilic, Chemoselective Reduces carboxylic acids > alkenes. Polar Aprotic

Important Named Reactions

Note: These reactions are crucial for converting between functional groups and for synthetic pathways in JEE Organic Chemistry.

1. Clemmensen vs Wolff-Kishner Reduction

Feature Clemmensen Reduction Wolff-Kishner Reduction
Reagents Zn-Hg / HCl NH2-NH2 / Base (KOH), Δ
Medium Acidic Basic
Intermediate Free Radical & Carbanion Carbanion
Use Converts carbonyl (aldehyde/ketone) to hydrocarbon under acidic conditions. Converts carbonyl to hydrocarbon under basic conditions.

2. Phenol-Specific Reactions

  • Reimer-Tiemann Reaction: Formylation of phenols using CHCl3 & NaOH → ortho-hydroxybenzaldehyde.
  • Kolbe’s Reaction: Phenol with CO2 under high T & P → ortho-hydroxybenzoic acid (Salicylic acid).
  • Cumene Hydroperoxide Rearrangement: Acid-catalyzed conversion of cumene hydroperoxide to Phenol + Acetone.

3. Other Key Reactions

  • Baeyer-Villiger Oxidation: Ketone + Peracid → Ester/Lactone.
  • Carbylamine Reaction: Test for 1° amines using CHCl3 & KOH → foul-smelling isocyanide.
  • Stephen’s Reduction: Nitrile + SnCl2/HCl → Aldehyde via imine intermediate.
  • Bouveault-Blanc Reduction: Ester + Na/EtOH → 1° Alcohol.
  • Mendius Reaction: Nitrile + Na/EtOH → 1° Amine.

Tests for Aldehydes

Test Reagent Positive Result Notes
Tollens’ Test Ammoniacal AgNO3 ([Ag(NH3)2]OH) Silver mirror (Ag0 deposit) Given by aldehydes & formic acid. Not by ketones.
Fehling’s Test Alkaline Cu2+ (CuSO4 + NaOH + Rochelle salt) Red ppt of Cu2O Only for aliphatic aldehydes. Aromatic aldehydes do not react.
Schiff’s Test SO2-treated rosaniline dye (colorless) Pink/Magenta color reappears Specific for aldehydes. Does not react with hemiacetals or ketones.

Core Concepts & Tips

JEE Focus: Mechanisms are important, but for quick problem-solving, remember reagent selectivity, product identification, and reaction conditions.

  • Grignard Reagent (RMgX): Source of carbanion (R-). Reacts as a strong base and nucleophile. Remember aqueous workup is essential after Grignard addition.
  • Oxidation of Alcohols: 1° → Aldehyde → Carboxylic acid. 2° → Ketone. 3° → No easy oxidation (no α-H).
  • Popoff’s Rule: During oxidation of an unsymmetrical ketone, the C–CO bond breaks such that the carbonyl stays with the smaller alkyl group.
  • Carbene (:CX2): Neutral, divalent carbon with 6 electrons. Acts as an electrophile. Generated from CHCl3 + OH-.
  • Organometallic Compounds: Contain C–Metal bond. More ionic character → more reactive (e.g., RMgX > R2CuLi).
